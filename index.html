Valentine's Day

Some intresting facts about Valentine's Day

Every year, more than 145 million greeting cards are exchanged on Valentine's Day, making it the second most popular holiday for cards after Christmas.

Red roses are the most popular flowers on Valentine's Day as they symbolize love. Chocolate is also the most popular gift, a tradition that started in the 19th century.

About 6 million people propose on Valentine’s Day each year!

  • The oldest known "valentine" dates back to 1415—it was written by the Duke of Orleans to his wife while he was imprisoned in the Tower of London.
  • Valentine's Day has several legends about its origin, but the most popular one tells the story of Saint Valentine, a priest who secretly married couples when the Roman Emperor Claudius II banned marriages for young soldiers.
  • Cupid (the Roman god of love) originates from the Greek god Eros, the deity of passion and love, who was initially depicted as a grown man but later became a cute winged boy.
  • Every year, around 1,000 letters are sent to Verona, Italy, addressed to Juliet, the heroine of William Shakespeare's tragedy.
